Wasih worked at Bison Transport (major Canadian carrier) and wants SRL to replicate the best parts of that enterprise structure while being carrier-friendly (Bison was carrier-side; SRL is broker-side but carrier-first).

**Bison Transport Org Structure (Reference Model):**
- Logistics teams divided by Canadian regions
- Logistics Coordinator + Assistants → reporting to Logistics Director
- In-house Track & Trace software built for logistics team
- Analytical team: market trends → fed to sales for rate negotiation
- Customer Service: CS Assistant → CS Rep → AE Assistant → Sales Executive (domestic + cross-border)
- Information Processing: uploaded invoices, driver logs, mail delivery to image lookup integrated with TMW (TMS)
- Accounting: Payroll, AR, AP → CFO; separate Credit team reviewing/approving customer accounts
- Compliance: reviewed carrier safety scores from RMIS
- Fuel team: bulk purchasing for better fuel prices
- HR involved across all systems

**SRL Strategic Direction:**
1. **Carrier-friendly self-service**: Easy onboarding, scorecard visibility, tier progression, revenue dashboards
2. **CPP (Caravan Partner Program)**: KPI-based differentiation — level of service, communication, on-time %, doc timeliness → tier qualification → bonus eligibility
3. **Carrier revenue visibility**: Monthly + weekly revenue, payment history, QuickPay access
4. **API integration with major broker houses**: CH Robinson, Echo, Coyote, XPO, TQL, Uber Freight, etc.
5. **Enterprise-grade organization**: regional teams, compliance, credit, analytics all functioning within the platform

**Why:** Wasih wants SRL to be THE most user-friendly website for carriers in the industry.

**How to apply:** Every carrier-facing feature should prioritize self-service, transparency, and ease of use. API integrations with other brokerages should let carriers see all their loads/payments across the industry in one place.
