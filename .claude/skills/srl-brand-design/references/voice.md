# SRL Voice and Naming

The way SRL writes. Carriers and shippers should hear a freight veteran on the line, not a marketing department.

---

## Core principle

SRL is run by people who have spent years in carrier operations. The writing should reflect that operational depth — verbs and numbers, lane-and-rate language, regulatory citations done right. Marketing softeners and adjective stacks signal the opposite to anyone who actually moves freight for a living, and they're an easy disqualifier in carrier and shipper conversations.

---

## Do

- **Lead with operational specifics**: lane (origin → destination), rate ($), transit (days), equipment (dry van 53', reefer, flatbed)
- **Use carrier and shipper vocabulary correctly**: PRO#, BOL, POD, detention, layover, NOA, MC#, DOT#, Carmack, FTL, LTL, dry van, reefer, deck
- **Stand behind decisions** — "We pay carriers in 7 days at 2% on Gold" is honest and bounded; "We try to pay carriers quickly when possible" is hedging
- Use **"carriers first"** framing for carrier-facing copy
- Use **"trust travels"** framing for shipper-facing copy

---

## Don't

- No marketing softeners: "best-in-class," "world-class," "synergy," "revolutionary," "disrupting," "next-generation," "AI-powered" (used as a sales adjective, not a technical descriptor)
- No padding adjectives — "comprehensive solution" → "platform"
- **No exclamation points in B2B documents.** Internal Slack is fine.
- **No emojis in formal SRL content** (BOL, RateCon, Invoice, BCA, QP, shipper email). Internal tools and casual messages are fine.
- **Don't fabricate metrics.** Pre-revenue means pre-revenue. "Trusted by leading shippers" is a lie if you have one customer in a pilot. Carriers and shippers can smell it.
- Don't write CTAs that beg. "Click here to learn more!" → "See lane coverage" or "Submit carrier packet."

---

## Calibrating the tone

Three questions to ask before publishing any SRL copy:

1. **Would a carrier dispatcher with 15 years of experience nod at this, or roll their eyes?** That's the audience floor. Anything that triggers an eye roll is wrong.
2. **Could a competitor say the same thing about themselves verbatim?** If yes, the copy isn't doing work for SRL specifically — it's just words. Replace with operational specifics that only SRL can claim.
3. **Is there a number, a lane, a regulatory citation, or a named tool somewhere?** Concrete details signal you actually do this work.

## Voice across surface types

| Surface | Posture |
|---|---|
| **Carrier-facing pages** (silkroutelogistics.ai/carriers, Caravan portal) | Direct, payment-first, respect the carrier's time. Lead with payment terms and load board access. |
| **Shipper-facing pages** (homepage, /shippers) | Trust-first, regulatory credibility, lane coverage. Lead with bond, insurance, MC#, lanes. |
| **Internal tools** (AE Console, Marco Polo) | Operational and technical — abbreviations OK, jargon expected, no consumer polish |
| **Legal documents** (BCA, BOL, QP, Rate Con) | Precise, regulatory-cited, no flourish. The "voice" is the absence of voice. |
| **Marketing collateral** (decks, one-pagers, social) | Quietly confident. Trust signals up front (MC, DOT, bond, insurance), then specifics. |
| **Email outreach** (shipper, attorney, partner) | Lead with operational signal (authority, lanes, capacity), close with a specific ask |

## Legal-document references — exact filenames

When referencing SRL legal documents in any artifact (proposal deck, email, web copy, code comments), use the canonical names:

- **Broker-Carrier Agreement Version 3.1 dated February 26, 2026**
  - File: `SRL_Broker_Carrier_Agreement_v3.1.docx`
- **Bill of Lading v2.9** (current PDF generator output)
- **Caravan Quick Pay Program Agreement Version 2.1**
  - File: `SRL_QuickPay_Agreement_v2.1.docx`

The version + date specificity is part of legal incorporation-by-reference. "The BCA" or "our agreement" is too vague to incorporate properly.

## Common rewrites — before / after

These are the patterns that come up most often in draft copy and need correcting.

**Before**: "We're excited to offer best-in-class carrier payment with industry-leading turnaround times!"
**After**: "Carriers on our Gold tier get paid in 7 days at 2%. Standard tier is Net-30."

**Before**: "Silk Route Logistics is a comprehensive freight solutions provider serving the supply chain needs of innovative shippers."
**After**: "SRL is a Michigan-licensed property broker (MC# 1794414, DOT# 4526880) specializing in CPG and wellness loads."

**Before**: "Our cutting-edge platform leverages AI to optimize carrier selection."
**After**: "Compass Engine scores carriers on FMCSA safety, on-time history, and lane fit. Compass score visible on every quote."

**Before**: "Looking forward to partnering with you on your transportation journey!"
**After**: "Quote attached for the Lebanon → Northlake lane. Pickup Tuesday, transit 3 days. Available to discuss."
