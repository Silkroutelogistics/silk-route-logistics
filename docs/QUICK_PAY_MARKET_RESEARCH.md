## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Quick Pay (QP) is a payment acceleration service where freight brokers pay carriers within 1-7 days of delivery (instead of the standard 30-45 days) in exchange for a fee of 1-5% of the invoice. SRL plans to deploy $70K of its own capital to offer Quick Pay at 1-3% fee rates as its first non-brokerage revenue stream, targeting $430K/year in QP fee revenue by Year 5.

This research validates the model: Quick Pay is a proven, low-risk profit center that top brokerages already operate. The fee structure, carrier demand, and technology platforms all support SRL's plan.

## 2. CARRIER ADOPTION & DEMAND

### Market Context

- **97% of US carriers have 10 trucks or fewer** -- these small carriers are the primary QP customer base
- **~60% of freight brokers use invoice factoring** to manage their own cash flow, indicating massive carrier demand for faster payments
- The freight factoring market reached **$200.76 billion in 2026** (projected $437.4B by 2035)
- Standard broker payment terms: **Net-30 to Net-45** (some stretch to Net-60+)
- Carriers typically wait **30-60 days** for payment under standard terms
- **Only 30% of owner-operators** have direct shipper contracts; the rest rely on brokers/load boards

### Adoption Estimates

While exact QP adoption percentages are not published industry-wide, the following indicators suggest very high demand:

- The **$200+ billion freight factoring market** exists specifically because carriers cannot wait 30-45 days for payment
- **90% of freight brokers** lack a web portal for carriers to view payment status (2019 TriumphPay survey), meaning QP differentiates SRL significantly
- Carriers need **$10,000-$15,000 in cash reserves** to survive the standard payment cycle -- many small operators don't have this
- Industry consensus from multiple sources: **a significant majority of small carriers will opt for QP when offered at reasonable rates** (1-3%)

### 2026 Trend: Some Carriers Pushing Back

A notable 2026 trend: some carriers are **refusing fast pay loads** when fees are excessive (4-5%+) or the terms are unclear. This validates SRL's strategy of keeping fees at 1-3% -- transparent, competitive, and fair.

## 4. FLOAT MANAGEMENT & CAPITAL STRATEGY

### The Core Challenge

When SRL offers Quick Pay, it pays the carrier in 1-5 days but doesn't collect from the shipper for 30-45 days. This creates a **25-40 day float** that SRL must fund.

### SRL's $70K Capital Allocation

With $70K dedicated to QP, SRL can support:
- At $2,000 average carrier payment: **35 loads outstanding** at any time
- At 30-day shipper collection cycle: supports about **$70K/month in QP payables**
- This is sufficient for **Year 1-2 volumes** based on the projections above

### Capital Recycling

The key insight: QP capital **recycles**. When shipper payments come in at Day 30, that capital is available to fund new QP payments. The $70K is not spent -- it's deployed and recovered continuously.

### Float Cost Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Capital deployed | $70,000 |
| Average float period | 30 days |
| Opportunity cost (7% annual) | $4,900/year |
| Year 1 QP revenue | $13,600 |
| **Net return on QP capital** | **$8,700 (12.4% ROI)** |
| Year 2 QP revenue | $42,500 |
| **Year 2 net return** | **$37,600 (53.7% ROI)** |

### Scaling Beyond $70K

As SRL grows, options to fund larger QP programs:
1. **Reinvest QP fees** back into the QP capital pool
2. **Business line of credit** -- draw to cover carrier QP, replenish as shipper payments arrive
3. **Factoring partnership** (e.g., Denim, OTR Solutions) -- they fund the QP, share the fee with SRL
4. **AP Financing** -- providers like OTR fund carrier payables on SRL's behalf

### Industry Benchmark

A brokerage with $30M in annual revenue that finances a 10-day cash gap ties up ~$820K in working capital. At 7% interest, that costs ~$58K/year. SRL's self-funded model avoids this entirely at early stages.

## 7. BEST PRACTICES FOR QP PROGRAMS

### From Denim and Industry Leaders

1. **Tiered Fee Structure**: Offer multiple speed tiers (next-day at 3%, 2-day at 2%, 5-day at 1%) so carriers choose their preferred cost/speed tradeoff

2. **Transparent Communication**: State QP terms clearly on every rate confirmation. Carriers increasingly refuse QP when terms are hidden or confusing.

3. **Carrier Vetting**: Only offer QP to carriers vetted through trusted programs (MCP, Highway, RMIS). This reduces fraud risk.

4. **Automation**: Use payment platforms (Denim Wallet) to automate QP fee calculation, carrier preference capture, and payment disbursement

5. **Default Opt-In with Opt-Out**: Set carriers to standard payment by default, let them opt into QP per load or as a blanket preference

6. **Monitor for Fraud**: Watch for unusual patterns -- new carriers immediately requesting QP on large loads

7. **Separate Accounting**: Track QP revenue as a distinct line item from brokerage margins

8. **Capital Reserve**: Maintain QP capital pool separate from operating funds

### What NOT To Do

- Do not charge more than 3% for 2-day payment -- carriers will refuse loads or use factoring instead
- Do not require long-term contracts for QP
- Do not mix QP fees into the load rate (non-transparent)
- Do not offer QP to unvetted carriers (fraud risk)

## 8. RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Shipper Non-Payment (Capital Exposure)

**Risk:** SRL pays the carrier via QP, then the shipper defaults on the invoice. SRL loses both the carrier payment and the QP fee.

**Mitigation:**
- Credit check all shippers before offering QP on their loads
- Set shipper credit limits
- Do not offer QP on loads from new/unvetted shippers
- Cap QP exposure per shipper at a percentage of their credit limit
- Reserve 5-10% of QP fee revenue as a bad debt provision

### Risk 2: Capital Depletion

**Risk:** Too many carriers opt for QP simultaneously, exhausting the $70K capital pool.

**Mitigation:**
- Set a monthly QP budget cap
- Prioritize QP for high-value, reliable carriers
- Implement a waitlist system when capital is fully deployed
- Plan for capital infusion triggers (line of credit at 80% utilization)

### Risk 3: Carrier Fraud

**Risk:** Double-brokered loads, fake PODs, or identity theft leading to fraudulent QP claims.

**Mitigation:**
- Vet carriers through MCP/Highway before QP eligibility
- Require verified POD before QP release
- Minimum carrier relationship period (e.g., 3 completed loads) before QP eligibility
- GPS/tracking confirmation of delivery

### Risk 4: Cash Flow Mismatch

**Risk:** Shipper payment cycles extend beyond 45 days, stretching SRL's float beyond capacity.

**Mitigation:**
- Negotiate shipper payment terms aggressively (Net-30 preferred)
- Factor invoices for slow-paying shippers
- Adjust QP availability based on shipper payment history
- Build in a capital buffer (20% above projected needs)

### Risk 5: Market Rate Compression

**Risk:** Competitors drop QP fees to 0-1%, squeezing SRL's revenue.

**Mitigation:**
- Differentiate on speed and reliability, not just price
- Bundle QP with other carrier benefits (fuel discounts, dedicated lanes)
- Lock in carrier loyalty through consistent QP performance
- Uber Freight already offers 0% QP on some loads -- but most carriers prefer reliability over price

## 9. REGULATORY CONSIDERATIONS

### FMCSA Requirements

- Freight brokers must maintain **$75,000 financial security** (surety bond or trust fund)
- If available financial security falls below $75K, FMCSA will **suspend operating authority** within 7 days
- Brokers must keep records of **freight charges collected and dates of payment to carriers** for 3 years
- New compliance date: **January 16, 2026** for all financial responsibility provisions
- QP programs themselves are **not separately regulated** -- they are a voluntary payment term between broker and carrier

### Tax Implications

- QP fees are ordinary business income
- QP capital deployed is a working capital asset, not an expense
- Interest on borrowed QP capital (if using a line of credit) is deductible
- Maintain clear books separating QP fee revenue from brokerage commission revenue

### Contractual Best Practices

- Include QP terms in the Broker-Carrier Agreement
- Specify: fee percentage, payment timeline, conditions (POD required, load completion verified)
- Include a clause allowing SRL to suspend QP availability at its discretion
- Ensure QP fee is clearly disclosed as a deduction from the carrier payment, not a separate charge
